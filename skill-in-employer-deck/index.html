SKILL-IN01 · The Problem

The market is broken
on both sides.

Companies
  • Job descriptions are wish lists, vague, generic, copied from last year
  • Resumes measure what people claim, not what they do
  • Home exams can be AI-generated or faked, with no signal on real work quality
  • Bad hire: 6-18 months of salary wasted before the mismatch surfaces
✕
Job Seekers
  • Certifications prove study time, not ability to execute
  • Portfolio projects are polished demos, staged and rehearsed, not observed
  • No signal for communication, delivery pace, or collaboration style
  • Organization fit: no way to know if the company's work style, culture, and pace actually match yours
Both sides are guessing. The market needs a common language, one that AI can read on both sides.
SKILL-IN02 · The New Unit

Not a resume. Not a job description.
A Skillset.

Company Skillset

AI-generated profile of what a specific role looks like at a specific company, reverse-engineered from how top performers actually work.

  • Work patterns: pace, consistency, collaboration style
  • Tech stack & tools: what they actually use day to day
  • Communication style: async vs sync, depth, meeting cadence
  • Culture signals: how ownership, initiative, and quality are expressed
  • Competency benchmarks: what "good" means here, not in general
  • Engineering patterns: commit velocity, PR success rate, vibe vs spec-driven delivery
"Senior Backend Engineer at Acme Corp" · the standard, not a job spec.
⟷
Candidate Skillset

AI-generated profile of how a person actually performs, built from months of real project work on the Skill-in platform.

  • Delivery record: how they perform under sprint pressure
  • Code quality & behaviour: commits, PR success rate, velocity, review patterns, AI-assisted vs spec-driven coding style
  • Communication behaviour: team engagement, stakeholder handling
  • Problem-solving approach: blockers, ambiguity, initiative
  • Role fit signals: PM, dev, designer, patterns across sprints
  • Work style fingerprint: pace, consistency, async vs sync, ownership signals
"Works like a mid-senior backend dev at a fast-moving product company."

SKILL-IN06 · The Employer Hook

The Employer Hook.

Two ways the right match finds you. Every Skillset in the platform is active — every candidate a potential match, whether they targeted you or not.

1
Scenario 01
Course Match Alert

A candidate takes a course shaped directly by your Skillset — built to your stack, your criteria, your work patterns. When they score above your threshold across all assessed dimensions, you receive an alert.

🏢 Your Skillset shapes the course criteria
↓
👤 Candidate completes it and scores high
↓
⚡ Alert — candidate matched your simulated work environment
This is intentional targeting. The candidate chose your environment. The signal is high-confidence by design.
OR
2
Scenario 02
Ambient Matching Engine

Every candidate who completes any course earns a Skillset that is stored in the platform. The matching engine continuously compares all candidate Skillsets against all employer Skillsets — across every role and every company. When a match clears the threshold, both sides are notified simultaneously.

👤 Candidate completes any course → Skillset stored
↓
⚙️ Matching engine runs continuously across all Skillsets
↓
⚡ Push notification → employer and candidate, simultaneously
This is passive discovery. You get matched to candidates who weren't even targeting your role — but whose profile fits.
